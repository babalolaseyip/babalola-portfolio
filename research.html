Research Profile

Machine Learning, Signal Processing, and Data-Driven Engineering Systems

My research focuses on developing reliable, interpretable, and computationally efficient methods for communication, sensing, and data-driven engineering systems operating under uncertainty. My work sits at the intersection of statistical signal processing, wireless communications, probabilistic modeling, and machine learning.

I develop models and computational pipelines that transform noisy, complex, and high-dimensional data into actionable engineering insight. This includes feature extraction, time-series modeling, probabilistic inference, simulation-driven validation, and machine learning approaches for classification, detection, prediction, and anomaly identification.

Research Themes

  • Machine learning for time-series and sensor data
  • Signal processing and low-SNR detection
  • Wireless communications and localization
  • Probabilistic modeling using HMM/HsMM
  • AI-driven engineering systems
  • Reliable and interpretable computational pipelines

Industry Relevance

  • Data science and model validation
  • AI systems evaluation and robustness analysis
  • Cybersecurity analytics and anomaly detection
  • Systems modeling and optimization
  • Scientific computing with Python and MATLAB
  • Applied R&D in complex engineering environments

Research Impact

My research has produced more than thirty peer-reviewed publications in signal processing, wireless communications, machine learning applications, and data-driven modeling. My work has applications in sensing, communication systems, environmental monitoring, localization, and intelligent systems.

Future Direction

My future work aims to expand the use of machine learning and emerging deep learning techniques for time-series, multi-modal, and sensor-driven engineering data. I am particularly interested in reliable AI systems that combine domain knowledge, interpretable modeling, and rigorous performance validation.
