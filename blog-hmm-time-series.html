Hidden Markov Models for Time-Series Analysis

Hidden Markov Models are useful for modeling time-series data when the actual system state cannot be observed directly. Instead, the state must be inferred from measurements that may be noisy, incomplete, or variable over time.

In signal processing and machine learning, HMMs are especially valuable for localization, detection, classification, and tracking. They allow researchers and engineers to model temporal dependence and estimate likely state sequences from observed data.

My research has applied HMM-based approaches to wireless localization, acoustic signal detection, and communication systems. In these applications, temporal modeling helps improve robustness by considering how signals evolve over time rather than treating each observation independently.

In industry, HMMs and related sequence models can support fraud detection, user behavior modeling, network anomaly detection, predictive maintenance, and sensor analytics. They remain valuable because they combine interpretability with strong mathematical foundations.
